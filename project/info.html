Information

Here are some of the things about that come about becoming an AFROTC Cadet:

  • Commissioning as a Second Lieutenant
  • Increasing your knowledge about the military
  • Learning about leadership
  • Opportunities to fly in planes
  • Opportinities to learn how to fly
  • Opportinities to attend Jump Training
  • Learning basic Drill & Ceremonies
  • Living a life that revolves around integrity, service before self, and exellence in all you do
  • Gaining physical strenght and mental toughness
  • Communication skills and teamwork

The first step is enrolling in our program. If you are interested please click on the interest form page and provide your information. Upon entering the program you will complete 2 years of General Military Course Training. Depending on your GPA, Commanders Ranking, Physical Fitness Score you will recieve a Enrollment Allocation(EA). EAs will allow cadets to attend Field Training for two weeks. This is where skills are tested. During these two weeks, you will be trained for the Professional Officer Course(POC). You will then be contracted as a cadet and will need to complete two more years of POC to become a commissiones officer. You must obtain a bachelors degree or you will not be allowed to commisson in the United States Air Force.
